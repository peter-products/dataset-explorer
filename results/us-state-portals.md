**Note:** "Custom" includes states with bespoke portals, transparency-focused sites, or portals where the underlying platform could not be confirmed. Several states without a unified portal still have department-level or GIS-specific portals.

## Key Findings

1. **Socrata (now Tyler Technologies) dominates** -- 21 of 50 states use Socrata for their primary open data portal, making it the clear market leader for state government open data.

2. **5 states have no unified portal** -- Arizona, New Hampshire, New Mexico, West Virginia, and Wisconsin lack a centralized state-level open data portal. Data is fragmented across individual agency sites.

3. **ArcGIS Hub is the go-to GIS platform** -- Nearly every state uses ArcGIS Hub for geospatial data, even when the primary open data portal runs on a different platform.

4. **New York leads in scale** -- With 3,000+ datasets on data.ny.gov, New York has the largest state-level Socrata deployment.

5. **Dataset counts are approximate** -- Many portals don't prominently display counts. Figures are estimates based on available information and should be verified directly.

6. **Platform consolidation is ongoing** -- Tyler Technologies acquired Socrata in 2018. Many portals still show "Socrata" branding but are now part of Tyler's "Data & Insights" division.
