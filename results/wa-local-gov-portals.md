# Washington State Local Government Open Data & GIS Portals

**Last updated:** 2026-04-10
**Research method:** Web search of all 39 WA counties and 26 cities with population > 50,000

---

## State-Level Resources (for context)

| Resource | URL | Platform | Notes |
|----------|-----|----------|-------|

## Key Findings

1. **ArcGIS Hub dominates.** The vast majority of WA local governments that have open data portals use Esri's ArcGIS Hub / ArcGIS Open Data platform. This is the de facto standard.

2. **Socrata is rare.** Only Seattle (data.seattle.gov) and King County (data.kingcounty.gov) use Socrata. These are the two largest and most sophisticated open data programs in the state.

3. **King County has the most comprehensive data.** Between the Socrata portal (tabular data) and ArcGIS Hub (GIS data), King County covers the broadest range of datasets. It also has specialized hubs (Climate Change GIS, State of GIS).

4. **Small/rural counties have minimal presence.** Adams, Asotin, Columbia, Ferry, Franklin, Garfield, and Whitman counties have very limited or no publicly accessible open data portals. Their data is typically available only by contacting the assessor or GIS department directly.

5. **Nearly all counties have assessor/parcel data online.** Even counties without formal open data portals typically have an online parcel viewer or assessor lookup tool, usually powered by ArcGIS or similar mapping platform.

6. **City portals cluster in King County suburbs.** Cities like Kent, Renton, Federal Way, Auburn, Bellevue, Kirkland, and Burien all have ArcGIS Hub portals, likely influenced by proximity to King County's GIS infrastructure and Esri's presence in Redmond.

7. **Tri-Cities (Kennewick, Pasco, Richland) all have ArcGIS Hubs.** This region has good open data coverage at the city level despite smaller populations.

8. **Some cities rely entirely on their parent county.** Sammamish relies on King County GIS, Spokane Valley on Spokane County GIS, Lacey on Thurston County, etc. These cities may not need their own portals.

9. **No CKAN instances found.** Despite CKAN being common nationally for open data, no WA county or city appears to use it.

10. **Data formats are consistent.** Most ArcGIS Hub portals offer CSV, KML, Shapefile (ZIP), GeoJSON, GeoTIFF, and PNG, plus API access via GeoServices, WMS, and WFS.
